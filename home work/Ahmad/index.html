Logar Districts

Logar Province - Districts Overview Logar Province is located in the eastern part of Afghanistan, just south of Kabul. It plays a strategic role due to its proximity to the capital. The province is known for its rich agricultural land, historical sites, and cultural heritage.

Pul-i-Alam District

  • 📍 Capital: Pul-i-Alam (also the provincial capital)
  • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Population: ~100,000+
  • 📝 Notes: Administrative center of the province, has major schools, clinics, and government offices.

Baraki Barak District

  • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Population: ~80,000
  • 📝 Notes: Known for tribal diversity and strong cultural traditions; has a long history of resistance and activism.

Charkh District

  • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Population: ~40,000
  • 🏔️ Terrain: Mountainous
  • 📝 Notes: Rich in natural beauty and known for traditional village life.

Kharwar District

  • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Population: ~30,000
  • 🏔️ Geography: Mountainous and forested
  • 📝 Notes: One of the more remote districts, famous for natural resources and forests.

Azr District

  • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Population: ~35,000
  • 🌾 Economy: Agriculture and livestock
  • 📝 Notes: Located far east in the province; bordering Nangarhar and Pakistan.

Mohammad Agha District

  • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Population: ~70,000
  • 🌿 Terrain: Fertile plains
  • 📝 Notes: Known for high agricultural production; many people commute to Kabul.

Khoshi District

  • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Population: ~45,000
  • 🌿 Terrain: Fertile plains
  • 📝 Notes: Smaller but agriculturally productive; culturally rich and peaceful./li>
